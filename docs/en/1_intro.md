# Before You Start

If you opened this file, you probably already suspect something's off. 
Maybe you've noticed yourself opening an app "just to check" and looking 
up forty minutes later. Maybe watching a ten-minute video without the 
urge to skip ahead has become physically hard. Maybe you just feel a 
strange emptiness after an hour of scrolling — like you ate a lot but 
somehow aren't full.

First thing I want to say: this isn't because you have weak willpower.

Short-form video — Reels, Shorts, TikTok — is built by teams of engineers 
and psychologists specifically to hold your attention for as long as 
possible. That's not an exaggeration or paranoia — it's baked directly 
into the business model: the longer you watch, the more ads you see. 
Your attention is the actual product being sold.

So trying to fight this with "willpower alone" is like trying to stop 
a car with your bare hands when the car was built specifically to keep 
moving. What works isn't willpower — it's system design: reducing 
availability, reshaping your environment, replacing habits. That's 
exactly what we're going to do.

## What's Ahead

This methodology is built as a sequence of steps:

First, understanding. You'll learn what's actually happening in your 
brain and why it pulls you back even when you're already tired of the 
endless feed.

Then, a week of observation. No restrictions — just noticing what's 
really going on.

After that, building friction. We won't just "delete the app" (that 
rarely sticks for long) — instead we'll build a system of barriers.

Then, replacement. The emptiness that shows up after cutting back has 
to be filled with something, or you'll end up right back where you 
started.

And finally, what happens after the first wave of motivation fades: 
how to avoid a long relapse, and how to live with all this long-term.

## One Important Note

This isn't a program about quitting your phone or social media 
entirely. That's unrealistic and, honestly, unnecessary. The goal is 
to get back control over when and how much you watch — not to turn 
the screen into forbidden fruit.

And one more thing: if you try this and slip up, that's part of the 
process, not a failure. There's no "clean streak" to protect here. 
There's only the next step you can take right now.
