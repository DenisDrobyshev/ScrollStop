# ScrollStop

I built this because I was the problem I'm writing about.

At some point I noticed I couldn't watch a video longer than three 
minutes without wanting to skip ahead. I'd open an app to check one 
thing and surface forty minutes later with no memory of what I'd 
actually watched. Reading a book felt like wading through mud. That's 
what pushed me to actually look into what short-form video does to 
attention — and to put together something concrete to fix it, not 
just for myself.

This is that something. It's free, it's open, and you're welcome to 
use it, copy it, fork it, or send back improvements.

## What This Actually Is

Not a willpower pep talk. Not "just delete the app." It's a step-by-
step method: first understand what's happening in your brain and why, 
then observe your own patterns for a week without changing anything, 
then gradually build friction and replace the habit with something 
else — with a whole section on what to do when you inevitably slip up, 
because you will, and that's fine.

## One Thing Before You Go In

This isn't about quitting your phone forever or feeling guilty every 
time you open TikTok. It's about getting to a point where opening the 
app is something you actually chose to do, not something that just... 
happened to you again.
