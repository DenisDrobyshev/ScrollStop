# How to Tell If This Is About You

Before reading the list below, let's agree on something: this isn't 
about diagnosing yourself and spiraling into panic. It's an honest 
inventory — like opening the fridge to see what's actually in there, 
not what you assumed was in there.

## What Happens With Behavior

You notice you open the app "for a minute" and close it thirty minutes 
or an hour later, with no memory of how that happened. You scroll the 
feed in the first seconds after waking up, before you've even sat up. 
You automatically reach for your phone in an elevator, in line, at a 
red light — even when a second earlier you weren't thinking about any 
video at all. You can't get through a clip longer than two or three 
minutes without wanting to skip or switch.

## What Happens With Attention

This one's probably the most unpleasant. Reading a long text — an 
article, a book — has become physically harder than it used to be. 
Not because you got dumber, but because your brain got used to 
stimuli changing every few seconds, and when the stimulus stops 
changing, something like an internal itch shows up. Holding focus on 
one task for more than ten minutes has become genuinely difficult. 
Sometimes you close the app and can't remember what you were even 
watching for the last five minutes — just a grey blur of images with 
no content.

## What Happens With Emotions

If you're cut off from the feed for a while — on a flight, in an 
important meeting, or just because your phone died — something like 
mild anxiety or irritation shows up. Boredom starts to feel unbearable, 
something that needs to be fixed immediately. After a long scrolling 
session, guilt or emptiness often follows — and, cruelly enough, that 
feeling frequently gets numbed by... another scrolling session.

## What Happens With the Body

Sleep problems, if you scroll lying in bed. Tension in your eyes and 
neck. Sometimes, the sensation that your phone buzzed, when it 
actually didn't.

## How to Work With This

There's no need to check boxes and tally a score like some magazine 
quiz. Just answer yourself honestly: how much of this is actually me, 
and how much is it getting in the way of the life I want to live. If 
the answer is "quite a lot, and quite badly" — good that you made it 
this far. Next: why this happens — and understanding that matters 
before changing anything.
