# On Relapses — Because They Will Happen

This is probably the single most important piece in the whole 
methodology, because this is exactly the point where most people give 
up entirely.

At some point you will open the app and lose an hour, even after two 
weeks of holding steady. This is not a program failure and not a 
personal failure. It's an expected part of changing any deeply-set 
habit — a lapse in attention, work stress, a bad day, plain fatigue — 
all of it lowers your ability to hold up the barriers you built, and 
that's normal for a human being, not a sign of weak character.

## The Main Trap After a Relapse

There's an effect in habit psychology sometimes called the 
"abstinence violation effect" — where after slipping up once, a 
person thinks "well I already blew it, doesn't matter now" and falls 
much further than they were before starting the work in the first 
place. One hour of scrolling turns into a whole lost evening, because 
the thought shows up: "I already ruined my progress, so it doesn't 
matter right now."

This is a thinking trap, not a fact. One hour doesn't erase two weeks 
of work, just like one bad meal doesn't erase a month of eating well. 
Progress isn't binary "worked / didn't work" — it's cumulative.

## What to Do Right After a Relapse

No need to beat yourself up — that doesn't help, it just raises stress 
levels, which is itself a trigger for the next relapse. Instead, three 
simple steps.

First — just notice the fact without judgment: "I spent an hour in 
the app." No "there I go again," no "I'm weak," just the fact.

Second — ask yourself what trigger fired right then. This is 
valuable information, not a reason for self-blame — you may have just 
found a trigger that wasn't on your original list.

Third — just go back to the plan from the next action, without 
waiting for Monday or a "fresh start." If it's three in the afternoon 
on a Tuesday, the next step starts now, not Monday.

## Longer Relapses

If you've slipped back into old habits for a week or more — don't 
start beating yourself up, and don't try to jump straight back to the 
same level of restriction you were at. Start over with a week of 
observation, but faster this time — because you already know your 
triggers and know which barriers already worked. A second attempt is 
almost always faster than the first.

## How to Tell If It's Actually Working

Don't compare yourself to some flawless version with zero relapses. 
Compare yourself a month ago to yourself now: how many hours on 
average went by then versus now, how many times a day you 
automatically opened the app then versus now. If the trend is going 
down — with setbacks, with relapses, but generally down — that's a 
process that's working.
