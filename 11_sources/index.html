    Sources¶

    Here are all the studies and data this methodology draws on. We deliberately link to original sources so anyone can verify and dig deeper.

    Attention and Its Decline¶

    Gloria Mark (UC Irvine), research 2004-2023 Multi-year tracking of how long people hold focus on one screen before switching. So back in 2004, we found the average attention span on any screen to be two and a half minutes on average. Throughout the years it became shorter. So around 2012 we found it to be 75 seconds. This is with logging techniques. And then in the last five, six years, we found it to average about 47 seconds, and others have replicated this result. Source: Gloria Mark, Attention Span: A Groundbreaking Way to Restore Balance, Happiness, and Productivity (2023); APA "Speaking of Psychology" podcast. 🔗 https://www.apa.org/news/podcasts/speaking-of-psychology/attention-spans

    Meta-Analysis: Short Video and Cognitive Function¶

    Nguyen et al., 2025, Psychological Bulletin (APA) The largest systematic review on this topic to date. This systematic review and meta-analytic investigation comprised data from 98,299 participants across 71 studies. Increased SFV use was associated with poorer cognition (moderate mean effect size, r = -.34), with attention (r = -.38) and inhibitory control (r = -.41) yielding the strongest associations. It also found that increased SFV use was associated with poorer mental health (weak mean effect size, r = -.21), with stress (r = -.34) and anxiety (r = -.33) showing the strongest associations. 🔗 https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41231585/ · DOI: 10.1037/bul0000498

    Internal TikTok Documents (US State Lawsuits, 2024)¶

    Investigation by 14 US state Attorneys General From documents unsealed by mistake in Kentucky's lawsuit. TikTok determined the precise amount of viewing it takes for someone to form a habit: 260 videos. After that, according to state investigators, a user "is likely to become addicted to the platform." While this may seem substantial, TikTok videos can be as short as 8 seconds and are played for viewers in rapid-fire succession, automatically.

    Important caveat from an independent researcher: the calculation that the attorney generals made... uses the most extreme estimate of a TikTok video, being as short as 8 seconds in length, when the average TikTok video in 2024 is actually 42.7 seconds — meaning the real time-to-habit is likely well over 35 minutes, though the fact the company had a precise internal calculation at all is telling on its own.

    The same documents also state: TikTok's own research states that "compulsive usage correlates with a slew of negative mental health effects like loss of analytical skills, memory formation, contextual thinking, conversational depth, empathy, and increased anxiety." 🔗 https://www.npr.org/2024/10/11/g-s1-27676/tiktok-redacted-documents-in-teen-safety-lawsuit-revealed

    EEG Study: The Neural Mechanism¶

    Frontiers in Human Neuroscience, 2024 Direct measurement of brain activity in people prone to short-video addiction. In the current study, we aimed to investigate the effect of short-form video use on attentional functions, measured through the attention network test (ANT). A total of 48 participants were recruited. The mobile phone short video addiction tendency questionnaire (MPSVATQ) and self-control scale (SCS) were conducted to assess the short video usage behavior and self-control ability. Electroencephalogram (EEG) data were recorded during the completion of the ANT task. 🔗 https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11236742/

    Opponent Process Theory¶

    Tian, Bi, Chen — Information Technology & People, 2023 An empirical study of TikTok users explaining the pleasure-withdrawal seesaw mechanism. This study conducts empirical research using data collected from 382 Chinese TikTok users. Based on the stimulus–organism–response framework, the research model was constructed from the opponent process theory (OPT) perspective through features and emotional elements. The results show that short-form video features influence addiction by activating users' perceived enjoyment and feeling of withdrawal. Based on the positive and negative reinforcement of the OPT, users must repeatedly interact with short-form videos to maintain positive emotions or reduce negative emotions. Eventually, this practice will lead to addiction. Additionally, users' procrastination moderates the relationship between feeling of withdrawal and addiction. 🔗 https://www.emerald.com/insight/content/doi/10.1108/itp-04-2020-0186/full/html · DOI: 10.1108/itp-04-2020-0186
